Eric Hamilton

Eric Hamilton is a trial attorney at Maples & Connick, LLC, where he handles traumatic brain injuries, nursing home negligence, and medical malpractice cases. Eric brings focused determination and meticulous case preparation to every client he represents, fighting to secure full compensation for individuals and families affected by catastrophic injuries.

Medical Malpractice

Medical malpractice litigation demands an attorney who can bridge the gap between complex medical evidence and the legal standards required to prove negligence. Eric reviews medical records with precision, consults with physicians and specialists in the relevant fields, and builds cases that clearly demonstrate how a healthcare provider’s failure to meet the standard of care caused harm. Whether the case involves a surgical error, a missed diagnosis, a birth injury, or a medication mistake, Eric prepares a thorough and compelling presentation for negotiation or trial.

Nursing home corporations have legal teams and insurance carriers working to minimize their exposure — Eric makes sure his clients have an advocate who matches that intensity.

Nursing Home Negligence

Eric also represents families of nursing home residents who have suffered abuse, neglect, or preventable injuries. He investigates staffing levels, training records, inspection histories, and corporate ownership structures to identify the systemic failures that allow harm to occur. Nursing home corporations have legal teams and insurance carriers working to minimize their exposure; Eric makes sure his clients have an advocate who matches that intensity.

Your Representation

Across all of his practice areas, Eric operates on a simple principle: thorough preparation leads to just outcomes. He prepares every case with the expectation that it will be tried before a jury, and that preparation shapes every decision from initial investigation through resolution. Eric is available through the firm’s offices in New Orleans, Mobile, and Atlanta. Consultations are free, and cases are accepted on a contingency fee basis.
