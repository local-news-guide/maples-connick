Civil Complaint Filed for the Deadly Mass Tragedy in New Orleans

Firm News

February 3, 2025

By Aaron N. Maples

Maples Connick has filed a civil complaint on behalf of victims and their families following the deadly mass tragedy event that occurred in New Orleans. This complaint seeks to hold accountable the parties whose negligence contributed to the devastating loss of life and the serious injuries suffered by dozens of individuals.

Our firm is committed to pursuing justice for those affected by this tragedy. We believe that the evidence will show that failures in security planning, barrier placement, and event management contributed to the harm inflicted on innocent people who were simply enjoying a public celebration.

The Civil Complaint

The civil complaint was filed in the United States District Court for the Eastern District of Louisiana. The complaint names multiple defendants, including municipal entities and agencies responsible for public safety planning and infrastructure in the area where the tragedy occurred.

The complaint alleges that the defendants were aware of the risks associated with large public gatherings in this area and had a duty to implement adequate safety measures. Despite prior warnings and established protocols for crowd protection, the complaint contends that the defendants failed to deploy sufficient physical barriers, security personnel, and surveillance systems to protect the public.

Specifically, the complaint alleges:

  • Failure to install adequate physical barriers at vulnerable access points along the route, despite the availability of bollards and other vehicle mitigation systems
  • Failure to implement proper traffic control measures to prevent unauthorized vehicle access to pedestrian areas during the event
  • Failure to provide adequate law enforcement presence at critical locations, despite intelligence suggesting elevated threat levels
  • Failure to coordinate effectively between municipal departments, law enforcement agencies, and event organizers regarding security protocols

Our Clients

We represent individuals who were physically injured during the tragedy as well as family members of those who lost their lives. The injuries our clients sustained range from traumatic brain injuries and spinal cord damage to broken bones, lacerations, and severe psychological trauma.

For the families who lost loved ones, no lawsuit can undo the damage that was done. But civil litigation serves an essential purpose: it demands an accounting. It forces the responsible parties to answer for their decisions — or their failure to act — and it provides a measure of financial security for families who have been devastated by loss.

Why Civil Litigation Matters

Criminal proceedings address the conduct of the individual perpetrator. Civil litigation addresses a different question: did the entities responsible for public safety do their jobs? Were the systems in place adequate? Were the known risks properly addressed?

These are questions that deserve answers. When a city, a police department, or an event organizer fails to implement reasonable safety measures, and people are harmed as a result, there must be accountability. Civil litigation is the mechanism through which that accountability is enforced.

Mass casualty events create complex legal issues, including questions of governmental immunity, multi-party liability, and the allocation of responsibility among numerous defendants. Maples Connick has the litigation experience and resources to navigate these complexities on behalf of our clients.

What Comes Next

The litigation process following a mass casualty event is lengthy and complex. The immediate steps include:

  • Service of the complaint on all named defendants
  • Preliminary motions and procedural matters
  • Discovery, including the production of internal documents, communications, and security plans
  • Depositions of officials, law enforcement personnel, and security consultants
  • Expert analysis of the security measures that were and were not in place

We anticipate that the discovery process will shed significant light on the decisions that were made — and the decisions that were not made — in the lead-up to this tragedy. We intend to pursue every available avenue to build the strongest possible case for our clients.
