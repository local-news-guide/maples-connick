Maples Connick to File Suit Against New Orleans and NOPD

Firm News

January 4, 2025

Maples Connick has filed a civil complaint against the City of New Orleans and the New Orleans Police Department (NOPD) for negligence leading to preventable harm. The complaint, filed on behalf of individuals who suffered serious injuries as a result of the city's and the department's failure to fulfill their public safety obligations, seeks full compensation for the damages our clients have endured.

This action reflects our firm's commitment to holding government entities accountable when their negligence causes harm to the people they are sworn to protect.

The Basis of the Complaint

The civil complaint alleges that the City of New Orleans and NOPD were negligent in their duty to provide adequate public safety measures. Specifically, the complaint contends that municipal officials and law enforcement leadership were aware of identifiable risks and failed to take reasonable steps to mitigate those risks.

Municipal negligence claims are governed by specific legal frameworks in Louisiana. Under Louisiana law, government entities owe a duty of care to the public. When that duty is breached — through inadequate staffing, poor planning, insufficient training, or failure to act on known threats — and the breach causes harm, the entity can be held liable.

The complaint identifies several specific failures:

  • Inadequate law enforcement deployment in areas known to present elevated public safety risks
  • Failure to implement security protocols that had been recommended by consultants and internal assessments
  • Communication breakdowns between departments that resulted in delayed or absent response to emerging threats
  • Failure to maintain and deploy physical infrastructure designed to protect public spaces from vehicular threats

Municipal Liability in Louisiana

Suing a government entity in Louisiana requires navigating specific procedural and substantive rules that do not apply to private defendants. Key considerations include:

Governmental Immunity

Louisiana law provides limited immunity to government entities for certain discretionary functions. However, this immunity is not absolute. When a government entity fails to carry out a ministerial duty — a duty that is clearly established by law or policy — immunity does not apply. The distinction between discretionary and ministerial functions is often the central legal question in municipal negligence cases.

The Duty-Risk Analysis

Louisiana uses a duty-risk analysis to determine negligence. The plaintiff must establish: (1) the defendant had a duty to conform to a specific standard of conduct; (2) the defendant breached that duty; (3) the breach was the cause-in-fact of the injury; (4) the breach was the legal cause of the injury; and (5) actual damages resulted. Each element must be proven by a preponderance of the evidence.

Notice Requirements

Claims against the City of New Orleans are subject to specific notice requirements. Failure to provide proper notice within the required timeframe can bar a claim entirely. Our firm ensures that all procedural requirements are met from the outset.

Our Clients

We represent individuals who suffered physical injuries and psychological trauma as a direct result of the failures alleged in the complaint. Their injuries include orthopedic trauma, soft tissue injuries, head injuries, and post-traumatic stress. Many of our clients have incurred substantial medical expenses, lost income, and continue to experience ongoing pain and limitations.

These are ordinary people who were going about their lives in a public space, relying on the city and its police department to provide the level of safety that every resident and visitor deserves. When that expectation was not met, they paid the price.

What We Are Seeking

The complaint seeks compensatory damages for our clients, including:

  • Past and future medical expenses
  • Lost wages and loss of earning capacity
  • Pain and suffering
  • Mental anguish and emotional distress
  • Loss of enjoyment of life

We will pursue every avenue of recovery available under Louisiana law to ensure that our clients receive the full measure of compensation they are entitled to.

The Importance of Accountability

Government entities operate with public funds and public trust. When those entities fail in their most basic obligation — keeping people safe — there must be accountability. Civil litigation serves that function. It demands transparency, compels the production of evidence, and ensures that failures are documented and addressed.

Our firm recognizes that litigation against a city and its police department is inherently complex. These are well-resourced defendants with experienced legal teams. But complexity is not a reason to avoid accountability. It is a reason to ensure that the plaintiffs are represented by attorneys with the experience and resources to match.
