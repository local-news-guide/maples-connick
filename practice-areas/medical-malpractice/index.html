Medical Malpractice Attorneys

Holding negligent healthcare providers accountable when preventable errors cause serious harm.

When a doctor, surgeon, nurse, or hospital fails to provide an accepted standard of care, the consequences can be devastating — permanent disability, chronic pain, or death. Medical malpractice cases are among the most complex areas of litigation. They require deep understanding of both the medicine and the law, extensive expert testimony, and the resources to take on hospitals and their insurers.

Maples Connick has the trial experience and technical knowledge to pursue medical malpractice claims across Louisiana, Alabama, and Georgia. Each state has distinct procedural requirements for bringing these claims, including pre-suit review panels, expert affidavit requirements, and damages caps. We handle these differences as part of our daily practice.

We work with leading medical experts to evaluate each case, establish the standard of care, and document how the deviation caused harm. If your case has merit, we pursue it aggressively — through negotiation when appropriate, and through trial when necessary.

Types of Cases

Medical Malpractice We Handle

Surgical Errors

Wrong-site surgery, retained instruments, nerve damage, and complications from procedures performed below the standard of care.

Misdiagnosis

Delayed or missed diagnoses of cancer, heart conditions, stroke, and infections that lead to worsened outcomes or preventable death.

Medication Errors

Wrong medications, incorrect dosages, dangerous drug interactions, and pharmacy dispensing errors that cause patient harm.

Birth Injuries

Cerebral palsy, Erb's palsy, brain injuries, and maternal complications caused by negligent prenatal care or delivery room errors.

Anesthesia Errors

Overdose, under-monitoring, allergic reactions, and airway management failures during surgical procedures.

Hospital Negligence

Understaffing, infection control failures, fall prevention lapses, and systemic institutional negligence.

Common Questions

Medical Malpractice FAQ

What qualifies as medical malpractice?
Medical malpractice occurs when a healthcare provider deviates from the accepted standard of care and that deviation causes injury. A bad outcome alone is not malpractice — there must be negligence. We work with medical experts to evaluate whether the standard of care was breached.
How long do I have to file a medical malpractice claim?
Deadlines vary by state. In Louisiana, the prescriptive period is one year from the date of discovery (La. R.S. 9:5628), with a three-year absolute limit. In Alabama, the statute of limitations is two years (Ala. Code § 6-5-482). Georgia allows two years (O.C.G.A. § 9-3-71). These are general guidelines — exceptions exist, so consult an attorney promptly.
Do medical malpractice cases always go to trial?
No. Many medical malpractice cases settle before trial. However, we prepare every case as if it will be tried. That preparation often leads to better settlement outcomes because the defense knows we are ready to present the case to a jury.
What damages can I recover?
Depending on your state and case, you may recover medical expenses, lost wages, future care costs, pain and suffering, and in some cases punitive damages. Louisiana caps general damages in medical malpractice cases — we can explain how this affects your specific claim.
How much does it cost to pursue a medical malpractice case?
We work on a contingency fee basis. You pay nothing upfront and nothing unless we recover compensation for you. We advance all case costs including expert fees, which can be substantial in medical malpractice cases.
