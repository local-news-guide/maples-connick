Birth Injuries

Protecting Families When Delivery Goes Wrong

Birth injuries caused by medical negligence are among the most emotionally devastating cases we handle. When a child suffers cerebral palsy, Erb’s palsy, hypoxic-ischemic encephalopathy, or other permanent injuries because a physician or hospital failed to meet the standard of care during labor and delivery, the consequences last a lifetime. These children may require around-the-clock care, specialized medical equipment, physical and occupational therapy, and educational support for decades.

Common causes of preventable birth injuries include failure to monitor fetal heart rate patterns, delayed decision to perform a cesarean section, improper use of forceps or vacuum extractors, failure to diagnose and treat maternal infections, and mismanagement of shoulder dystocia. Each of these situations involves a critical window where proper medical intervention could have prevented the injury.

Birth injury cases require specialized medical and economic expertise. We retain pediatric neurologists, neonatologists, and obstetric experts to establish the standard of care and causation. We also work with life care planners and economists to calculate the full cost of caring for an injured child over their expected lifetime — costs that often reach millions of dollars.

In Louisiana, the medical review panel process applies to birth injury claims. The prescriptive period is one year from discovery, with a three-year outer limit. Alabama and Georgia allow two years. These deadlines can be complex in birth injury cases because some injuries are not immediately apparent. Contact Maples & Connick at 504-269-3870 for a free consultation.

Cases We Handle

Types of Birth Injury Cases

Cerebral Palsy

Brain damage caused by oxygen deprivation during labor and delivery. Often results from failure to monitor fetal distress, delayed C-section, or umbilical cord complications.

Erb's Palsy / Brachial Plexus

Nerve damage to the shoulder and arm caused by excessive force during delivery, typically during shoulder dystocia. Can result in permanent weakness or paralysis of the affected arm.

Oxygen Deprivation

Hypoxic-ischemic encephalopathy (HIE) caused by interruption of oxygen supply during birth. Minutes of oxygen deprivation can cause permanent brain damage or death.

Delayed C-Section

Failure to perform a timely cesarean section when fetal monitoring indicates distress. Delays measured in minutes can mean the difference between a healthy child and permanent brain injury.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my child's birth injury was preventable?

Many birth injuries result from medical negligence rather than unavoidable complications. Warning signs include failure to respond to abnormal fetal heart rate patterns, prolonged labor without intervention, excessive force during delivery, and failure to perform a timely C-section. An attorney can obtain your medical records and have them reviewed by obstetric experts.

What is a life care plan in a birth injury case?

A life care plan is a comprehensive document prepared by a medical and rehabilitation expert that projects all future care needs and costs for an injured child. It includes medical treatment, therapy, assistive devices, home modifications, educational support, and attendant care — calculated over the child's expected lifetime. These plans are critical for ensuring adequate compensation.

Is there a deadline to file a birth injury claim?

Yes, but the deadlines vary by state and may be extended for minors. In Louisiana, the prescriptive period is generally one year from discovery with a three-year outer limit. Alabama and Georgia allow two years. Because birth injury discovery can be delayed, consult an attorney as soon as you suspect negligence contributed to your child's condition.
