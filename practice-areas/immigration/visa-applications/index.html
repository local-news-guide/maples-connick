Visa Services

Navigating the Visa Process

The United States visa system is complex and unforgiving. A single error on an application, a missing document, or a missed deadline can result in denial, delays, or loss of status. At Maples & Connick, our immigration attorney Chanel Payne guides clients through every step of the visa process — from initial petition through approval — in English, Spanish, and Portuguese.

We handle a wide range of visa categories including H-1B specialty occupation visas, L-1 intracompany transfer visas, O-1 extraordinary ability visas, E-2 treaty investor visas, F-1 student visas, and B-1/B-2 visitor visas. Each category has specific eligibility requirements, documentation standards, and processing timelines that must be carefully managed.

For employer-sponsored visas, we work directly with petitioning employers to prepare Labor Condition Applications, demonstrate specialty occupation requirements, and compile supporting evidence packages. For student and visitor visas, we help applicants prepare for consular interviews and address potential grounds of inadmissibility.

Our offices in New Orleans, Mobile, and Atlanta serve immigrant communities across the Gulf South and Southeast. Call 504-269-3870 to schedule a consultation.

Cases We Handle

Types of Visa Cases

Work Visas (H-1B, L-1, O-1)

Specialty occupation visas for professionals, intracompany transfers for multinational employees, and extraordinary ability visas for individuals with exceptional achievement in their field.

Student Visas (F-1)

Student visas for academic programs at U.S. universities and colleges, including OPT/CPT work authorization, SEVIS compliance, and change of status applications.

Investor Visas (E-2, EB-5)

Treaty investor visas for entrepreneurs starting or purchasing U.S. businesses, and immigrant investor visas for substantial capital investment creating U.S. jobs.

Visa Renewals & Extensions

Timely renewal and extension petitions to maintain valid status, including change of status applications when transitioning between visa categories.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a visa application take?

Processing times vary significantly by visa category and USCIS service center. H-1B petitions typically take 3-6 months (or 15 business days with premium processing). Student visa consular appointments depend on embassy wait times. We monitor processing times and use premium processing where available to minimize delays.

Can I work in the U.S. while my visa is being processed?

It depends on your current status and the type of visa you are applying for. Some applicants can continue working under their current authorization while a new petition is pending (such as H-1B cap-gap extensions). Others must wait for approval. We advise each client on their specific work authorization options during the application process.

What happens if my visa application is denied?

A denial is not necessarily the end. Depending on the grounds for denial, options may include filing a motion to reopen or reconsider, submitting a new petition with additional evidence, or appealing to the Administrative Appeals Office. We analyze every denial to determine the strongest path forward.
