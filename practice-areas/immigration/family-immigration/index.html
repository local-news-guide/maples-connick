Family Immigration

Keeping Families Together

Family reunification is a cornerstone of U.S. immigration policy. U.S. citizens and lawful permanent residents can petition for certain family members to immigrate to the United States or adjust their status to permanent residence. At Maples & Connick, we handle the full range of family-based immigration cases — from fiance visas to parent petitions to hardship waivers.

The family immigration process begins with filing an I-130 Petition for Alien Relative with USCIS. The petitioner (the U.S. citizen or permanent resident) must establish a qualifying family relationship. Processing times and visa availability depend on the relationship category and the beneficiary's country of birth. Immediate relatives of U.S. citizens (spouses, parents, and unmarried children under 21) have no annual quota and process fastest.

We also handle K-1 fiance visas (for engaged couples where one partner is a U.S. citizen), VAWA self-petitions (for spouses and children who are victims of domestic violence by a U.S. citizen or permanent resident), and I-601A provisional unlawful presence waivers (which allow certain applicants to request forgiveness for unlawful presence before departing for consular processing).

Cases We Handle

Types of Family Immigration Cases

Spouse & Fiance Visas

I-130 spousal petitions, K-1 fiance visas, and CR-1/IR-1 immigrant visas for spouses of U.S. citizens. Includes interview preparation and evidence of bona fide marriage.

Parent & Sibling Petitions

I-130 petitions for parents and siblings of U.S. citizens. Parent petitions are immediate relative category (no wait); sibling petitions fall under the fourth preference with longer wait times.

VAWA Self-Petitions

Protection for spouses and children who are victims of abuse, battery, or extreme cruelty by a U.S. citizen or permanent resident. VAWA allows victims to self-petition without the abuser's knowledge or cooperation.

Unlawful Presence Waivers

I-601A provisional waivers for applicants who accrued unlawful presence and must demonstrate that denial would cause extreme hardship to a qualifying U.S. citizen or permanent resident relative.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to bring a family member to the U.S.?

Immediate relatives of U.S. citizens (spouses, parents, unmarried children under 21) can typically complete the process in 12-18 months. Other family preference categories have longer wait times — married children and siblings of citizens may wait 7-20+ years depending on their country of birth due to annual visa quotas.

Can I petition for my spouse if they are undocumented?

In many cases, yes. If your spouse entered the U.S. legally and is eligible for adjustment of status, they may be able to obtain a green card without leaving the country. If your spouse entered without inspection, the process is more complex and may require an I-601A waiver and consular processing. We evaluate each situation individually.

What is a VAWA self-petition?

VAWA (Violence Against Women Act) allows spouses and children who are victims of abuse by a U.S. citizen or permanent resident to file their own immigration petition without the abuser's involvement. The abuser is never notified. VAWA self-petitioners can obtain work authorization and eventually a green card independently of their abuser.
