Motorcycle Crashes

Riders Deserve Equal Justice

Motorcycle accidents produce some of the most catastrophic injuries we see. Without the protective shell of a car, motorcyclists are exposed to the full force of impact — resulting in road rash, compound fractures, traumatic brain injuries, spinal cord damage, and amputations at rates far higher than other motor vehicle crashes. According to the NHTSA, motorcyclists are 29 times more likely to die in a crash than passenger car occupants per mile traveled.

Beyond the severity of injuries, motorcycle accident victims face a unique challenge: bias. Insurance adjusters and jurors often assume the motorcyclist was speeding, weaving through traffic, or acting recklessly — regardless of the facts. This bias can reduce settlement offers and influence jury verdicts. At Maples & Connick, we confront this prejudice head-on by building evidence-driven cases that focus on the negligent driver's conduct, not stereotypes about motorcyclists.

All three states where we practice — Louisiana, Alabama, and Georgia — require motorcycle helmets for all riders. Helmet use (or non-use) can affect damages calculations, but it does not eliminate the negligent driver's liability for causing the crash. We handle the legal complexities while you focus on recovery.

Cases We Handle

Types of Motorcycle Accident Cases

Left-Turn Accidents

The most common type of fatal motorcycle crash: a car turns left in front of an oncoming motorcycle at an intersection. The driver fails to see the motorcycle or misjudges its speed.

Road Rash & Severe Burns

High-speed contact with pavement causes deep abrasion injuries that may require skin grafts, leave permanent scarring, and carry risk of infection. Road rash can be as debilitating as a burn injury.

No-Contact Crashes

A car changes lanes, swerves, or opens a door without seeing the motorcyclist, forcing the rider to lay down the bike or crash to avoid impact. The negligent driver may leave the scene without realizing what happened.

Wrongful Death

Fatal motorcycle crashes leave families devastated. We pursue wrongful death claims against negligent drivers, trucking companies, and government entities responsible for dangerous road conditions.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Will the insurance company blame me because I ride a motorcycle?

They will try. Insurance adjusters routinely argue that motorcyclists are inherently reckless, regardless of the facts. We counter this bias with accident reconstruction evidence, witness testimony, dashcam and surveillance footage, and expert opinions that focus on the negligent driver's conduct — not stereotypes about riders.

Does helmet use affect my motorcycle accident case?

All three states where we practice require helmets. If you were wearing a helmet, it strengthens your case. If you were not, the defense may argue that your head injuries would have been less severe with a helmet. However, failure to wear a helmet does not eliminate the other driver's liability for causing the crash itself.

What if the driver who hit me left the scene?

Hit-and-run crashes are unfortunately common in motorcycle accidents. If the driver cannot be identified, you may still recover compensation through your uninsured motorist (UM) coverage. We pursue all available sources of recovery, including UM claims and any available surveillance footage to identify the at-fault driver.
