Our Practice

Fighting for the Seriously Injured

Catastrophic personal injuries can have profound and life-altering effects on victims and their families. At Maples & Connick, we represent people who have suffered the most devastating injuries — traumatic brain injuries, spinal cord damage, amputations, severe burns, and other life-changing harm caused by someone else's negligence. These are not minor fender-bender cases. These are cases where everything is at stake: your ability to work, to care for your family, and to live the life you had before the accident.

Our attorneys handle personal injury claims across Louisiana, Alabama, and Georgia, with offices in New Orleans, Mobile, and Atlanta. Each state has its own legal framework governing negligence, liability, and damages. Louisiana operates under a civil law system with different procedural rules than the common law systems in Alabama and Georgia. We understand these distinctions and how they affect your case — from filing deadlines and comparative fault standards to the types and amounts of damages available in each jurisdiction.

We also handle wrongful death claims on behalf of families who have lost a loved one because of another party's reckless or negligent conduct. Whether the death was caused by a motor vehicle collision, a defective product, a workplace disaster, or medical negligence, we pursue accountability from every responsible party. Our attorneys work with medical experts, accident reconstruction specialists, economists, and life-care planners to build cases that reflect the full scope of what has been lost.

Maples & Connick operates on a contingency fee basis. You pay nothing upfront and owe no attorney fees unless we recover compensation for you. If you or a family member has been seriously injured, call 504-269-3870 for a free consultation with a trial attorney who will evaluate your case honestly and fight for the result you deserve.

Common Questions

Personal Injury FAQ

How much does it cost to hire a personal injury attorney?

Nothing upfront. Maples & Connick works on a contingency fee basis, which means you pay no attorney fees unless we recover compensation for you. The consultation is free, and there are no hourly charges or retainer fees. Our fee is a percentage of the recovery we obtain on your behalf.

How long do I have to file a personal injury claim?

Every state sets its own deadline — called the statute of limitations — for filing a personal injury lawsuit. These deadlines vary by state and by the type of claim. Missing the deadline can permanently bar your right to recover compensation. Contact us as soon as possible after an injury so we can preserve your rights and begin building your case while evidence is still available.

What types of compensation can I recover?

Depending on your case, you may be entitled to compensation for medical expenses (past and future), lost wages and diminished earning capacity, pain and suffering, emotional distress, loss of enjoyment of life, and in some cases, punitive damages. In wrongful death cases, family members may recover funeral and burial costs, loss of financial support, and loss of companionship.

What if I was partially at fault for my injury?

You may still be able to recover compensation even if you were partially at fault. Louisiana, Alabama, and Georgia each apply different rules to shared fault situations. Some states reduce your recovery by your percentage of fault, while others may bar recovery if your fault exceeds a certain threshold. Our attorneys will evaluate the specific facts of your case under the applicable state's law.

Do I have to go to court, or will my case settle?

The majority of personal injury cases settle before trial, but the strength of your settlement depends on the other side believing you are willing and prepared to go to court. At Maples & Connick, we prepare every case as though it will be tried to verdict. That preparation is what drives fair settlements — and when a fair offer does not come, we take the case to trial.
