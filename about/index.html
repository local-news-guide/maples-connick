About Maples Connick

Trial attorneys who take your case — and your fight — personally.

Our Story

Maples & Connick was founded on a straightforward principle: injury victims deserve attorneys who prepare every case as though it will go to trial. Aaron Maples and Brendan Connick built this firm to handle the kinds of catastrophic injury cases that demand preparation, resources, and courtroom experience.

With offices in New Orleans, Mobile, and Atlanta, we represent clients across Louisiana, Alabama, and Georgia. Our team of six attorneys and a dedicated paralegal handles personal injury, medical malpractice, nursing home negligence, and immigration cases.

We take a focused caseload. That means every client gets direct access to their attorney — not a case manager, not a paralegal screening calls. When you hire Maples Connick, you get attorneys who know every detail of your case because they are working it themselves.

What We Believe

Insurance companies, corporations, and institutions have legal teams on retainer. They prepare for litigation as a cost of doing business. The people they injure through negligence deserve attorneys who match that preparation — and exceed it.

We believe the courtroom is where accountability happens. Not every case goes to trial, but every case should be prepared as if it will. That preparation changes how the other side evaluates your claim, and it changes what they offer to resolve it.

What Defines Us

Our Values

Four principles that guide how we prepare, communicate, and litigate on behalf of every client.

Preparation Over Posturing

We build cases on evidence and expert analysis, not rhetoric. Every claim is investigated thoroughly before we ever make a demand — because preparation is what wins cases, not bluster.

Direct Access

Your attorney answers your calls. Every time. We do not route clients through layers of staff. When you have a question about your case, you speak directly with the lawyer handling it.

Honest Assessment

We tell you what your case is worth — and what it will take to get there. No inflated promises. No misleading projections. You deserve a clear-eyed evaluation from day one.

Trial Readiness

Every case is prepared for trial. That is not a tagline — it is how we work. Insurance companies settle differently when they know your attorney has tried cases to verdict and is willing to do it again.

How We Work

Our Approach to Every Case

From the first phone call to final resolution, every step is deliberate. Here is what working with Maples Connick looks like.

Listen First
We start by listening. You tell us what happened. We ask the questions that matter. Within that first conversation, we give you an honest assessment of whether you have a viable claim — at no cost and no obligation.
Investigate Deeply
We gather medical records, accident reports, expert opinions, and witness statements. We reconstruct what happened, identify every responsible party, and document the full scope of your injuries and losses.
Build the Case
Every case file is built as though it will be presented to a jury. We retain qualified experts, prepare demonstrative evidence, and develop a litigation strategy tailored to the specific facts and applicable law in your state.
Negotiate or Try
We pursue full compensation through demand and negotiation. If the other side refuses a fair settlement, we take your case to trial. We never pressure clients to accept less than their case is worth because we are unwilling to go to court.
Resolve and Recover
You receive your compensation. We handle all liens, subrogation, medical billing negotiations, and distributions — so you can focus on your recovery, not paperwork.

Where We Practice

Multi-State Coverage

We litigate across three states with meaningfully different legal frameworks. These differences matter, and we handle them every day.

Louisiana

Our headquarters is in New Orleans. Louisiana operates under a civil law system derived from the Napoleonic Code — a fundamentally different legal framework than the common law states around it. We navigate those distinctions daily.

Alabama

Alabama applies contributory negligence — one of the strictest fault standards in the country. If you are even partially at fault, recovery can be barred entirely. Our attorneys know how to build cases that survive that standard.

Georgia uses modified comparative fault with a 50% bar. We handle complex injury and wrongful death cases in the Atlanta metro area and throughout the state, working with local experts and understanding Georgia-specific procedural requirements.
